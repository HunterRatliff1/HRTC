How this app works

Sidebar

The sidebar (to the left) can be used to filter the data displayed across the app. Any active filters will apply ACROSS THE WHOLE APP. Keep this in mind as filtering in this sidebar might prevent data from loading elsewhere (e.g. filtering out all of the Question Type = Quiz questions prevent the graph from loading on the Questions page if the Message selected under "Select Text Message" is a quiz)

Towards the bottom of the sidebar is there is an option to filter by certain variables (e.g. gender, race, grade). Selecting the variable on the filter by dropdown allows you to filter by that variable in the box below. Changing the filter by selection (e.g. from gender to race) clears the previous filter (in this case, gender), preventing the user from filtering down to groups small enough to reveal potentially identifying information (e.g. 10th grade black males from FBISD).

Descriptive Statistics page

This page is pretty straightforward, as it shows the composition of the users in the campaign by demographic groups. It includes options to fliter out people who didn't respond to the demographic questions (i.e. NA), as well as an option to include/exclude users who have opted out of the campaign.

Have Answer page

Types of messages ("Question types")

Broadly, there are a few categories of message to be aware of when viewing data from this app.

Much of this app focuses on text messages sent to users that have a "correct answer", such as quizzes (Your partner stops by your job despite telling them that it made you uncomfortable. Is this 1-healthy, 2-unhealthy, or 3-abusive?) or T/F (Fact or Crap? As long as your partner is not hurting you or calling you names, your relationship is healthy. Reply 1-Fact or 2-Crap).

Some texts have an optional response (e.g. Knowledge w/ EX, Hotline) where users are asked to reply with "1" to receive another message with more info / an example. These questions do not have a "correct answer" and may not be included in figures/tables.
